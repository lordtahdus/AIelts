Revised Essay:

"What stands out when gauging success is the amount of money and material possessions. However, I am convinced that affluence is not the best measure of success, and other factors such as personal satisfaction and self-awareness are more important.

There are two main reasons why wealth cannot be used as a reliable indicator of success. Firstly, wealth can be obtained effortlessly. In other words, if money is not acquired through hard work, people from privileged backgrounds should not be automatically admired. For example, individuals who inherit their wealth from their families do not necessarily achieve personal success. Secondly, it is widely acknowledged that some significant accomplishments in life may yield a high level of reward either too late or not at all, particularly among artists and scientists throughout history.

Nevertheless, people can achieve success by gaining personal satisfaction and self-awareness. In terms of satisfaction, individuals can thrive regardless of their financial status, as long as they develop useful skills. They can enhance their abilities, giving them a competitive advantage in either academic or professional fields. A clear example of this is obtaining one's dream job and finding deep fulfillment in life. Furthermore, people can gain a better understanding of themselves and the essence of life by working on their inner feelings, even in a world that prioritizes material success, which is often superficial.

In conclusion, a person's success does not necessarily correlate with luxury items or great wealth. Instead, I strongly believe that those who have a strong understanding of themselves and strive to find personal satisfaction are the true embodiment of success."
