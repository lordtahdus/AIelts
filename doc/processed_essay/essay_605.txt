Topic:

"There is an increasing trend around the world of married couples deciding not to have children, Discuss the advantages and disadvantages for couple who decide to do this."

Essay:

"Marriage is still considered a big achievement for some people in some countries with the main aim being to have children as their offspring. However, in several parts of the world, there is a phenomenon that married couples taking the decision not to have children during their life. In this essay, I will outline the benefits and drawbacks of this issue in our society’s life.

To begin with, being childfree will give more time for married people to focus on their job to achieve higher positions in the workplace which will lead to higher productivity and their financial condition will be more stable to satisfy daily needs. Besides, this decision will reduce overpopulation in some countries which is the main source of social and economic problems such as poverty and famine because of lack of access to higher education and healthcare, for instance in India and China. The statistic shows that only 5% of the population in these countries have the opportunity to pursue higher education which leads to a huge economic gap.

Nevertheless, this decision will give adverse consequences for the economic condition because the availability of the workforce will be reduced and lead to steady economic growth in the long run. For example, in Japan, most of their population is dominated by those above 50 years and in the last 20 years, their economic growth is less than 2% a year because of the shortage of labour in various economic sectors since married couples deciding not to have children.

In conclusion, I contend that childfree has more disadvantages than its advantage because it will cause detrimental effects on the economic growth of a country in the long run due to a lack of labour in various economic sectors."


Revised:

Marriage is still considered a significant milestone for many individuals around the world, with the primary expectation of starting a family. However, there is a growing trend in certain regions where married couples are choosing to remain childless. In this essay, I will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this decision in contemporary society.

To begin with, one major advantage of being childless is that it enables married individuals to dedicate more time and energy to their careers, ultimately leading to higher work positions and increased productivity. Consequently, their financial stability improves, providing them with the means to meet their daily needs comfortably. Moreover, this decision can address the issue of overpopulation in certain countries. Overpopulation can lead to various social and economic problems such as poverty and scarcity of resources. Countries like India and China, for example, currently struggle to provide adequate access to education and healthcare due to their high population density. By choosing not to have children, married couples contribute to reducing the strain on these resources and promote a more balanced society.

However, there are also significant disadvantages to not having children. One notable drawback is the negative impact it can have on a country's economy in the long term. With a reduced workforce, economic growth becomes stagnant. Japan offers a prime example, where the majority of the population is over 50 years old. Over the past two decades, Japan's economic growth has hovered around a mere 2% annually due to a shortage of labor in various sectors. This demonstrates how the decision not to have children can hinder a country's economic progress.

In conclusion, while choosing to remain childless may have some advantages, such as increased focus on career growth and resource management, the disadvantages outweigh the benefits. The long-term consequences, particularly on the economy, can be detrimental due to a decreased workforce. Therefore, in the larger context of societal well-being and progress, it is crucial to encourage married couples to consider having children, ensuring a sustainable future for all.
