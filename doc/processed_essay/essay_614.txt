Topic:

"These days the fashions in clothing are constantly changing. Is this affecting individuals and the environment in a positive or a negative way? Give reasons for your answer and include relevant examples from your knowledge and experience."

Essay:

"In the ever-changing world, alteration in the fashion industry is no exception. However, such continuous change can negatively impact the people and the surroundings by increasing their unnecessary expenditure and excessive resource consumption respectively.

Individuals mostly prefer to be in trend with fashion in order to imitate celebrities. This habit not only can lead to extravagant spending but can also develop the habit of showing off which can bleakly affect during the lows of one’s life. In addition, people begin forgetting their inner personality and start believing that up-market clothes can foster their self-confidence. To exemplify, under peer pressure, teenagers are highly cautious about their dresses and often try to wear the latest designs to attract attention. Hence, the above reason and example vividly illustrate the effect on individuals.

The constant production of wearables requires a great amount of resources. Although high demand in the market is good for the  economy, the increased requirement surge the demand for raw materials which affects the ecosystem in multiple aspects. Firstly, tons of water are needed to grow cotton which is the most prominent ingredient for the production. Secondly, in order to fulfil the requirement, acres of fields are essential which can ultimately decrease the portion of eatable crops. Finally, the cotton crop reduces the fertility of the region which makes it unusable for agriculture for a longer duration. As per the report issued by Agricultural Ministry, Calcutta has the largest barren land because of the high amount of cotton production. Thus, the aforementioned reasons substantiate how fashion badly affects the environment.

To conclude, the increased urge to purchase fashionable attires not only affects human beings by causing financial burden but also negatively influences the ecosystem by using immense water, diminishing food crops and degrading the health of the land."


Revised:

In the ever-changing world, alterations in the fashion industry are becoming the norm. However, this constant change can have a negative impact on individuals and the environment by increasing unnecessary expenditure and excessive resource consumption, respectively.

Many individuals feel compelled to keep up with the latest fashion trends in order to imitate celebrities. This desire not only leads to extravagant spending but also fosters a habit of showing off, which can have detrimental effects during times of financial difficulty. Moreover, people may lose touch with their true selves and start believing that expensive clothes will enhance their self-confidence. For instance, teenagers, influenced by peer pressure, often feel the need to wear the latest designs in order to attract attention. Thus, it is evident that these factors can have a negative impact on individuals.

The constant production of fashionable clothing requires a significant amount of resources. While high demand in the market can benefit the economy, it also leads to an increased demand for raw materials, which can have multiple negative effects on the ecosystem. Firstly, the production of cotton, a key ingredient in clothing, requires vast amounts of water. Secondly, large areas of land need to be used to grow the cotton, which can encroach upon the space available for edible crops. Lastly, cotton crops can deplete the fertility of the land, rendering it unsuitable for agriculture for an extended period of time. According to a report issued by the Agricultural Ministry, Calcutta has the largest area of barren land, largely due to the high volume of cotton production. Therefore, it can be clearly seen that the fashion industry negatively impacts the environment.

In conclusion, the increased desire to purchase fashionable clothing not only affects individuals by causing financial burdens but also has negative effects on the ecosystem through the excessive consumption of resources. This includes the depletion of water sources, the reduction of available land for food crops, and the degradation of soil health. It is essential for individuals and the fashion industry to find sustainable alternatives that minimize these negative impacts and promote a more environmentally-friendly approach to fashion.
