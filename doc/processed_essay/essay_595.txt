Topic:

"Some people get into debt by buying things they don’t need and can’t afford.

What are the reasons for this behaviour?

What action can be taken to prevent people from having this problem?"

Essay:

"Nowadays, some individuals have financial problems because they overuse their money by consuming goods that are useless for them. This essay will discuss the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide practical solutions to address the issue.

People purchase consumptions that they do not need and get into debt because they lack basic knowledge of personal asset management. That is because the education system we have in the contemporary era only focuses on theoretical knowledge without providing basic tools for students to live their daily lives, leading to a sufficient sense of investment and saving after they grow up. Therefore, people tend to use every quarter in their pocket for consumption without considering their financial situations, and this can harm them in the long term as they need to work twice hard to pay back their debts and have no time for further developments in both personal and professional settings. For example, according to a survey about college graduates in China, the major problem of their school curriculums is the lack of basic finance and investment courses, which may prevent them from over-consumption after they graduate.

To solve the problem, the general public, financial institutions and governments should act together in an endeavour. On an individual level, people can use the Internet to self-study related knowledge about asset management and proper consumption behaviours. In addition, banks and other financial authorities should publish more strict regulations to loan money to people for consumption, which can prevent people from over-consumption in the early stage. Finally, governments should restrict education systems by adding financial courses into curricula, teaching students from primary school, and developing their purchase hygiene gradually. For instance, the Japanese government forced all the elementary schools to set up a pocket money course in which students can learn about the different ways to use their money and the according results, which was highly commented by citizens.

In conclusion, some people struggle with their consumption debts for useless things because they have insufficient knowledge about asset management and have no plan for their consumption in advance. All the individuals by learning financial knowledge, banks by setting strictly examining progress of loan, and governments by adjusting curriculums and adding consumption practices for students can help to address the issue."


Revised:

Nowadays, many individuals find themselves in financial difficulties due to their habit of purchasing things that are neither essential nor affordable. This essay will discuss the reasons behind this behavior and propose practical measures to counter it.

One of the main reasons why people get into debt by buying unnecessary items is their lack of basic financial management knowledge. Modern education systems tend to prioritize theoretical subjects, neglecting the need to equip students with essential life skills such as budgeting and saving. As a result, individuals are inclined to spend their money on immediate gratification without considering their financial stability. This can have long-term repercussions, as these individuals are then burdened with debt and have little room for personal and professional growth. For example, a survey conducted in China on college graduates revealed that a lack of financial and investment courses in their curriculum contributed to their tendency to overspend.

To address this issue, concerted efforts are required from various stakeholders, including the general public, financial institutions, and governments. On an individual level, people should take the initiative to self-educate themselves on topics such as asset management and responsible consumption through readily available resources, such as the Internet. Financial institutions, too, play a crucial role by implementing stringent regulations when lending money for non-essential purchases, thereby discouraging excessive spending. Finally, governments should intervene by incorporating financial literacy courses into school curricula from a young age, gradually instilling good purchasing habits. For instance, the Japanese government has made it mandatory for elementary schools to have a pocket money course that educates students on the various ways to utilize money and the potential consequences of their choices.

In conclusion, the prevalence of debt due to unnecessary purchases can be attributed to a lack of financial knowledge and foresight. By empowering individuals with financial education, encouraging responsible lending practices, and introducing practical consumption training for students, this issue can be effectively tackled. It is only through a collective and proactive approach that we can prevent people from falling into the trap of unsustainable debt.
